How a seat becomes a booking

This visual explains the happy path in plain language. A traveler finds a flight, picks a seat, the system temporarily protects that seat, and then the hold becomes a confirmed booking.

1

Find a Flight

The traveler searches for a route and date. The backend returns flights that match the trip.

Flight SCHEDULED
2

Choose a Seat

The traveler views seats for that flight. Seats that are free can still be selected.

Seat AVAILABLE
3

Hold the Seat

The system locks the seat so two people cannot grab it at the same time.

Seat HELD Hold ACTIVE
4

Confirm Booking

If the hold is still active and not expired, the system creates the booking record.

Reference BOOK-XXXXXX
5

Seat is Booked

The seat is no longer available to others, and the hold is marked as completed.

Seat BOOKED Hold CONFIRMED

What this means

The system treats a seat like scarce inventory. It does not just save a booking immediately. It first protects the seat with a temporary hold, then turns that hold into a confirmed booking.

Why it matters

When many people try to book the same flight, correctness matters. The backend locks the seat during important steps so only one person can successfully reserve it.

AVAILABLE means open HELD means temporarily protected BOOKED means confirmed
